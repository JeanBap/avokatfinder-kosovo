Tatimi i Transferit të Pronës

Property transfer tax payable on real estate sales in Kosovo

Tatimi i transferit të pronës is the tax levied on the transfer of real property ownership in Kosovo. The tax is assessed on the higher of the declared transaction price or the market reference value determined by the Kosovo Tax Administration (Administrata Tatimore e Kosovës — ATK).

The current standard transfer tax rate in Kosovo is 0.5% of the assessed transaction value, making it one of the lowest property transfer taxes in the Western Balkans. The tax is payable by the seller, although parties often negotiate allocation by contract.

How It Works in Practice

Payment must be completed before the notary will certify the purchase contract. The buyer and seller present proof of tax payment (or confirmation of tax exemption) to the notary at the signing appointment. Without this proof, the notary cannot proceed with certification.

Certain transfers are exempt from transfer tax in Kosovo, including transfers between spouses, transfers to and from the state or municipal government, transfers made pursuant to court orders, and in some cases first-home purchases under specific programmes. Exemptions should be confirmed with the ATK before relying on them.

Key Facts

Rate0.5% of the higher of declared price or ATK reference value
PayerSeller (parties may negotiate)
TimingMust be paid before notary certifies the contract
ATK AuthorityKosovo Tax Administration — may reassess under-declared prices
ExemptionsSpousal transfers, state transfers, court orders, some first-home programmes

If the ATK determines that the declared price is below the market reference value, it will reassess the tax base upward and issue a tax assessment notice. Buyers and sellers who deliberately under-declare prices face fines and the reassessed tax amount plus interest.

Common Mistake: Under-declaring the sale price to reduce transfer tax is illegal and easily detected by the ATK, which maintains its own market reference values by location. The penalty plus interest can exceed the tax saving.
Expert Tip: Before signing, confirm with the ATK what reference value they hold for the property. If the reference value is higher than your agreed price, factor the difference into the tax calculation and budget accordingly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the transfer tax rate in Kosovo?

The standard rate is 0.5% of the higher of the declared sale price or the ATK's market reference value for the property.

Who pays the transfer tax?

The seller is ordinarily liable, but the parties can contractually agree to share or shift the obligation. The ATK primarily pursues the seller.

What if the ATK's reference value is higher than the sale price?

The ATK will apply the higher reference value as the tax base. Under-declaration attracts additional fines and interest on the shortfall.
