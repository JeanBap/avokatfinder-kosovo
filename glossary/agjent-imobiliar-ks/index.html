Agjent Imobiliar (Real Estate Agent)

A real estate agent or broker facilitating property transactions in Kosovo

Agjent imobiliar is a real estate agent or broker who facilitates the buying, selling, and renting of property in Kosovo. Kosovo's real estate agency sector has grown rapidly since 2005, driven by urbanisation and remittance-driven property demand from the large diaspora community.

Kosovo does not yet have a fully developed licensing regime for real estate agents equivalent to those in Western Europe. While agents are required to register their businesses and comply with general business law, specific professional certification requirements for real estate agents are limited. Buyers should exercise caution when selecting an agent.

How It Works in Practice

Real estate agency fees in Kosovo are typically paid by the seller, though in some transactions the buyer also contributes a fee. Commission rates vary but commonly range from 1% to 3% of the transaction price. Fee arrangements should be confirmed in writing before the agent undertakes any work.

Kosovo's diaspora community is a significant driver of property purchases, and many transactions involve buyers living abroad who rely heavily on agents and local representatives. Buyers purchasing remotely should appoint a trusted legal representative (avokat) in addition to the agent, as the agent's legal duty of care may be limited.

Online property portals have expanded rapidly in Kosovo, with platforms such as MerrJep, Kimeta, and others listing residential and commercial properties. While useful for market research, listings should be verified for accuracy, as price and availability information is not always current.

Common Mistake: Buyers relying solely on a real estate agent without appointing a separate legal representative (avokat) are exposed to significant risk in Kosovo, where agent duties are not codified. The agent's interests may not always align with yours.
Expert Tip: If buying remotely, grant a limited power of attorney (prokurë e kufizuar) to a trusted Kosovo-based avokat rather than the agent, to handle due diligence and registration. This separates the commercial and legal roles.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do real estate agents in Kosovo need a licence?

Kosovo does not have a comprehensive agent licensing regime. Agents must register their businesses but formal professional certification requirements are limited. Verify the agent's reputation through references.

How much is the agent commission in Kosovo?

Typically 1%–3% of the transaction price, usually paid by the seller. Always confirm the fee arrangement in writing before engaging the agent.

Should I use an avokat as well as an agent?

Yes, especially if buying remotely. The agent handles marketing and negotiations; the avokat conducts due diligence, reviews contracts, and manages registration. Their roles are distinct and complementary.
