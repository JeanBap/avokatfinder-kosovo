Pronësia Bashkë (Co-ownership)

Shared ownership of real property by two or more persons in Kosovo

Pronësia bashkë refers to co-ownership, a situation where two or more persons hold ownership rights over the same piece of real property in Kosovo. Co-ownership arises most commonly through inheritance (where multiple heirs inherit a property), joint purchase, or the dissolution of a marriage.

Each co-owner holds a defined fraction (pjesë) of the property, which they can in principle sell, mortgage, or transfer independently. However, the other co-owners typically have a right of pre-emption (e drejta e parablerjes) — the right to purchase the selling co-owner's share at the same price offered by a third party before the sale proceeds to that third party.

How It Works in Practice

Decisions about the joint use, management, and improvement of co-owned property ordinarily require the agreement of all co-owners. Disputes among co-owners about the management or disposal of property are common in Kosovo and can be resolved by a court, which may order the partition (ndarja) of the property or its sale with proceeds divided proportionally.

Buyers purchasing from one co-owner of a property should be aware of the right of pre-emption held by the other co-owners. If the pre-emption right is not properly observed, the other co-owners may challenge the transaction in court.

Buying co-owned property where some co-owners are deceased and their shares have not been formally inherited is particularly risky. The inheritance proceeding (procedura e trashëgimisë) must be completed and all heirs registered as co-owners before a clean sale can proceed.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes purchase a share from one co-owner without serving formal notice of pre-emption on the others. The other co-owners can then challenge and potentially unwind the transaction. Always ensure pre-emption rights are properly waived in writing before completing the purchase.
Expert Tip: If purchasing a property with multiple co-owners — common with inherited properties in Kosovo — instruct your legal adviser to verify that all co-owners are accounted for, all inheritance proceedings are complete, and all co-owners sign the sale agreement.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the right of pre-emption in Kosovo co-ownership?

Co-owners have the right to purchase a selling co-owner's share at the same terms offered to a third party before the sale proceeds. Failure to observe this right can allow co-owners to challenge the transaction.

Can a co-owner sell their share without the others' consent?

In principle yes — a co-owner can sell their fraction — but they must first offer it to the other co-owners at the same price (right of pre-emption). Only if the other co-owners decline can the share be sold to a third party.

What happens when co-owners cannot agree on property management?

Either co-owner can apply to the Kosovo courts, which may order partition of the property into separate parcels or, if partition is not practical, order a sale with proceeds divided proportionally.
