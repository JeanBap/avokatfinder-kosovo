E Drejta e Ndërtimit (Building Right)

The legal right to construct a building on land owned by another party in Kosovo

E drejta e ndërtimit is a real property right that allows the holder to construct and own a building on land that is owned by another person or entity. This right is conceptually similar to a superficies right in other European legal systems and is regulated under Kosovo's Law on Property and Other Real Rights.

The building right is created by a notarised agreement between the landowner and the right-holder and is registered in the property rights register. Once registered, it can be bought, sold, mortgaged, or inherited independently of the underlying land ownership.

How It Works in Practice

Building rights are particularly relevant in Kosovo's urban development context, where state or municipal land is sometimes made available to developers on a long-term building right basis rather than through outright sale. Buyers purchasing apartments in such developments should understand that they own the apartment but the building right (not the freehold land) underpins the development.

The term of a building right is agreed between the parties and registered in the rights register. Upon expiry, the building may pass to the landowner unless the agreement provides for removal or renewal. Buyers should review the term and expiry conditions carefully before purchasing a property subject to a building right.

When a building right expires or is terminated, the legal situation of the property and any structures on it must be resolved per the terms of the underlying agreement and Kosovo property law. Buyers should seek legal advice before purchasing any property where the title is based on a building right rather than freehold ownership.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes purchase apartments in developments built on a building right without realising the land is not owned freehold. Always check the title base — is it freehold ownership or a building right? If the latter, check the remaining term and expiry conditions.
Expert Tip: Ask the developer or seller for the underlying building right agreement and check its term, renewal options, and expiry provisions before committing to purchase. A legal adviser can help you assess the implications.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the building right (e drejta e ndërtimit) in Kosovo?

It is a real property right allowing the holder to construct and own a building on land belonging to another party. It is registered in the property rights register and can be transferred or mortgaged.

Is a building right the same as freehold ownership?

No. With freehold, you own both the building and the land. With a building right, you own the building but the land belongs to someone else, and the right has a defined term.

What happens when a building right expires?

The building may pass to the landowner, or the parties may renew the right or agree on removal of the building, depending on the terms of the original agreement.
