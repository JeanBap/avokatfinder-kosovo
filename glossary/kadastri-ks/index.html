Kadastri (Cadastre)

The official registry of land parcels and property boundaries in Kosovo

Kadastri is Kosovo's official cadastral register, maintained by the Kosovo Cadastral Agency (Agjencia Kadastrale e Kosovës — AKK). It records the physical characteristics of every real estate parcel: location, area, boundaries, land use classification, and building footprints.

The cadastre is the primary technical reference for any real estate transaction in Kosovo. Before purchasing property, buyers should obtain a cadastral extract (certifikata kadastrale) confirming the parcel's registered area, boundaries, and land use, and verify it against what is visible on site.

How It Works in Practice

Kosovo's cadastral system underwent significant reform after 1999, with assistance from international donors to rebuild records that had been damaged or destroyed. While coverage has improved substantially, some rural or disputed areas may have incomplete or contested records, requiring additional due diligence.

The AKK operates municipal cadastral offices across Kosovo where buyers and their representatives can request certified extracts and cadastral plans. Many municipalities also offer online access to basic cadastral information, though certified copies must still be requested in person or through authorised agents.

Discrepancies between the cadastral record and the property rights register (Regjistrimi i Pronës) are not uncommon in Kosovo, particularly for older properties. Any inconsistency should be investigated and resolved before completing a purchase, as it can indicate boundary disputes, illegal construction, or administrative errors.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes rely only on the property rights register and skip the cadastral extract. In Kosovo, where the two systems can diverge, checking both is essential — the cadastre confirms the physical reality while the rights register confirms legal ownership.
Expert Tip: Request the cadastral extract and cadastral plan on the same day you begin due diligence, and update it immediately before signing. Boundaries and area figures in older documents may have been superseded by subsequent surveys.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the AKK?

The Kosovo Cadastral Agency (Agjencia Kadastrale e Kosovës) is the government body responsible for maintaining the cadastral register of all real property in Kosovo.

What does a certifikata kadastrale show?

It shows the parcel's registered area, boundaries, land use classification, building footprints, and any flags or notes on the parcel's status.

Are cadastral records complete for all of Kosovo?

Coverage is extensive but not universal. Some rural areas and areas affected by post-1999 displacement may have incomplete or contested records; additional due diligence is needed in such areas.
