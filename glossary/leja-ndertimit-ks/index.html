Leja e Ndërtimit

The building permit required for lawful construction in Kosovo

Leja e ndërtimit is the administrative permit issued by the relevant municipal authority authorising construction of a new building or significant modification of an existing one in Kosovo. Construction without a valid building permit is illegal and carries serious consequences for both builder and subsequent buyer.

The permit is issued by the municipal directorate for urban planning and environment (drejtoria komunale e urbanizmit dhe mjedisit). Applications must include architectural plans approved by a licensed engineer, a site location plan, proof of property ownership or building right, and payment of the administrative fee.

How It Works in Practice

Kosovo has faced a widespread problem of illegal construction (ndërtim pa leje), particularly during the post-1999 reconstruction period. The government has run several legalisation programmes under the Law on Legalisation and Special Urbanisation of Buildings, but many structures remain unlegalised.

Before completing a purchase, buyers should verify: (1) the building permit (leja e ndërtimit), (2) the use permit (leja e përdorimit) confirming the completed structure meets the approved plans, and (3) whether any extensions or modifications were made after the original permit. All three documents should match the current state of the property.

Key Facts

Issuing AuthorityMunicipal directorate for urban planning (drejtoria komunale e urbanizmit)
Use PermitLeja e përdorimit — required before lawful occupation
Legalisation LawLaw on Legalisation and Special Urbanisation of Buildings
EnforcementMunicipal building inspectorate (inspektorati i ndërtimit)
Buyer RiskDemolition orders for unlegalised structures

Purchasing an unlegalised structure exposes the buyer to demolition orders issued by the municipal inspectorate (inspektorati i ndërtimit). Even where legalisation is in progress, the outcome is not guaranteed. Buyers should not rely on a seller's assurance that legalisation will be completed after the sale.

Common Mistake: Buyers frequently accept a seller's word that an unlicensed extension is 'in the process of legalisation'. In Kosovo, legalisation applications can be refused or stalled indefinitely. Never purchase without verifying the current legal status of all structures on the property.
Expert Tip: Request certified copies of the building permit, use permit, and any legalisation application documents before the due diligence deadline. If the use permit is missing, require it as a condition of completion.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the leja e ndërtimit?

It is the building permit issued by the municipal urban planning directorate, authorising construction of a new building or significant alteration of an existing one.

What is the leja e përdorimit?

It is the use permit issued after construction is completed, confirming the structure complies with the approved plans and is fit for occupation.

What is ndërtim pa leje?

It means construction without a building permit. Such structures are illegal and exposed to demolition orders from the municipal inspectorate.
