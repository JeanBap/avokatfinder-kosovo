Hipoteka (Mortgage)

A real estate security right used to secure loans in Kosovo

Hipoteka is a security right over real property used as collateral for a loan in Kosovo. When a borrower takes a mortgage loan from a bank, the bank registers a hipoteka over the property in the cadastral rights register. The borrower keeps possession and use of the property, but the bank can enforce the security through sale if the borrower defaults.

A mortgage in Kosovo must be established by a notarised agreement and registered in the property rights register to be valid against third parties. Kosovo's banking sector is regulated by the Central Bank of Kosovo (Banka Qendrore e Kosovës — BQK), which supervises mortgage lending standards.

How It Works in Practice

Kosovo's mortgage market has grown substantially since 2010, driven by a young population and rising urbanisation. Banks typically offer fixed and variable-rate products in euros, as Kosovo uses the euro as its official currency despite not being an EU member state.

Upon full repayment, the lender issues a discharge document (deklaratë e fshirjes) which the borrower submits to the cadastral office to remove the mortgage from the rights register. Buyers purchasing a mortgaged property should insist that the existing mortgage is discharged simultaneously with the transfer of ownership.

Key Facts

CreationNotarised mortgage agreement + cadastral rights registration
CurrencyEuro (Kosovo uses EUR as official currency)
RegulatorCentral Bank of Kosovo (BQK)
DischargeDeklaratë e fshirjes filed at cadastral office after repayment
EnforcementThrough Kosovo courts; judicial auction on default

Enforcement of a defaulted mortgage in Kosovo proceeds through the Kosovo courts, typically resulting in a judicial auction of the property. Kosovo has implemented enforcement reforms to speed up the process, but court proceedings can still take months to years depending on the complexity of the case.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes complete a purchase without checking whether the property carries a registered mortgage. Always request a certified property rights extract on the day of signing — any registered hipoteka will appear there.
Expert Tip: If buying with a mortgage, coordinate with your bank to ensure the seller's existing mortgage discharge and your new mortgage registration happen at the same notary appointment. This simultaneous exchange protects both parties.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does a mortgage need to be notarised in Kosovo?

Yes. The mortgage agreement must be notarised and the mortgage registered in the cadastral rights register to be enforceable against third parties.

What currency are Kosovo mortgages in?

Kosovo uses the euro as its official currency, so all mortgage loans are denominated in euros.

What is the deklaratë e fshirjes?

It is the lender's discharge document issued after full loan repayment. The borrower submits it to the cadastral office to remove the mortgage from the property rights register.
