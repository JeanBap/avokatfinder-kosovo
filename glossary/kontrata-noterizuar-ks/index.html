Kontrata e Noterizuar

A notarised contract required for valid real estate transactions in Kosovo

Kontrata e noterizuar is a contract that has been certified by a licensed Kosovo notary, making it an authentic legal document. For real estate transactions — purchases, mortgages, gifts, and long-term leases — notarisation is mandatory under Kosovo law.

The notary's role is to verify the identities of both parties, confirm their legal capacity and free consent, read and explain the document's content, and certify that the transaction complies with Kosovo law. The notarised contract then forms the basis for property rights registration.

How It Works in Practice

Kosovo's notarial system was established in 2009 and is regulated by the Law on Notary (Law No. 03/L-010). The Kosovo Chamber of Notaries (Dhoma e Notarëve të Kosovës) licenses and supervises notaries, who operate across Kosovo's municipalities.

Notary fees for real estate transactions are set by the Kosovo Chamber of Notaries based on a tariff schedule related to the transaction value. For a typical residential purchase, the notary fee is modest relative to the overall transaction cost. Both parties typically attend the signing appointment simultaneously.

Original RetainedBy the notary; certified copies issued to parties

Buyers should select a notary with jurisdiction in the municipality where the property is located. The notary retains the original certified contract in their protocol and provides certified copies to both parties. These certified copies are used for tax filings, mortgage applications, and land registration.

Common Mistake: Using a private written agreement without notarisation is a common mistake for smaller transactions or preliminary agreements. An unnotarised contract cannot be used to register ownership and may not be enforceable if disputed.
Expert Tip: Bring all required documents to the notary appointment — valid identity documents for both parties, cadastral extract, property certificate, and any mortgage documentation — to avoid delays or adjournments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is notarisation required for all property sales in Kosovo?

Yes. The purchase contract must be notarised by a licensed Kosovo notary for the transaction to be valid and for ownership registration to proceed.

Who regulates notaries in Kosovo?

The Kosovo Chamber of Notaries (Dhoma e Notarëve të Kosovës) licenses and supervises all notaries in Kosovo.

Can I use any Kosovo notary for my transaction?

Notaries have territorial jurisdiction, so you should use a notary based in the municipality where the property is located, or confirm that the chosen notary has jurisdiction for that area.
